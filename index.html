About

Broadly, I like problems where mathematics, computation, and reliability collide: Can AI help us prove things? Can learned models be trusted inside scientific solvers? Can we make mathematical software that is both useful and rigorous?

Research

My work is mostly about building computational tools that do math carefully.

Formal verification and Lean 4

I work on AI-assisted formalization, automated theorem proving, and proof evaluation in Lean 4. I care about proofs that are not only kernel-accepted, but readable, faithful, and useful.

Mathematical AI

I am interested in machine learning systems that can reason about mathematics, search through libraries, and help with theorem proving.

Scientific computing

Through LLNL/RIPS-IPAM, I worked on derivative-finding methods for constitutive laws used in finite element solvers. Fitting the data is not enough; if derivatives are bad, Newton solvers will notice.

Problems That Fascinate Me

These are the problems I keep coming back to.

Conjecture generation and significance

A mathematician can conjecture, often selecting one of many conjectures as their intuition tells them this problem will motivate interesting new mathematics. Can AI systems create conjectures of the same importance? How do you measure the significance of a conjecture?

Formal and informal mathematics

How can we effectively and transparently bridge the gap between formal and informal mathematics?

Fast matrix multiplication

Given two n×n matrices, can they be multiplied in O(n²) arithmetic operations?
